About Semeion

The idea behind Semeion comes from a genuine curiosity about how brands operate and what motivates people to choose one brand over another.

It is undeniable that brands shape trends and influence culture, but the relationship is reciprocal. We, as consumers, influence those changes just as much. Every purchase, recommendation, and conversation feeds back into the way brands evolve. That exchange has always fascinated me.

More specifically, I have always been curious about why certain brand decisions work on people. Not in a "marketing tips" kind of way, but in a how did that name, that colour, that one small formal choice get inside my head? kind of way. Why does one campaign become part of the cultural conversation while another disappears? Why does one product feel like it belongs to us before we have even used it?

That curiosity naturally led me to psychographics. People rarely buy things simply for what they do. They buy them for what choosing them says about who they are, or who they aspire to be. A brand that truly understands this stops selling a product and starts selling an identity. I find that mechanism endlessly fascinating, in much the same way that, once you understand story structure, you begin to notice it everywhere.

My background is in film criticism, scriptwriting for YouTube channels with a large combined audience, and translation and interpreting. During the 2022 refugee crisis, I also worked as an interpreter in refugee camps across Moldova and Romania. At first glance, these fields may seem unrelated. To me, they are connected by the same instinct. They all require paying close attention to language, narrative, and human behaviour, and asking why one particular choice resonates more deeply than another.

Semeion is where that instinct is directed towards brands, consumer behaviour, films, books, campaigns, and culture. The aim is not simply to explain what happened, but to understand why it happened. Some articles take the form of shorter observations on branding, PR, or cultural moments. Others are research-based essays that explore psychographics, consumer behaviour, symbolism, and storytelling through detailed case studies.

Sometimes the subject is a product. Sometimes it is a story. Sometimes it is a campaign or a public reaction. The question underneath is always the same.

Why did this specific choice work, and what does it reveal about the psychology behind it?

That is what Semeion is here to explore.
